# State of AI Report

> **Source:** [State of AI Report](https://www.stateof.ai/), an annual
> open-access report published since 2018.
> **Curators:** **Nathan Benaich** (AI investor) and **Air Street Capital**.
> **Format:** Annual ~200-slide deck + supplementary materials (compute
> index, practitioner survey, ~25-min video overview, blog post commentary).
>
> This page is a **homelab index of the report archive** — a stable
> pointer to where each year's edition lives, with the six recurring
> dimensions the report covers. The reports themselves are the
> reading; this page just makes them browsable alongside the homelab's
> other reading materials.

## Why this page exists in our Reading section

The State of AI Report is to "where the field is" what the
[a16z AI Canon](a16z-ai-canon.md) is to "what the field built": an
annual, opinionated, citation-heavy snapshot. Two complementary
shapes:

| | a16z Canon | State of AI Report |
|---|---|---|
| Cadence | Static (May 2023 snapshot) | Annual since 2018 (8 editions through 2025) |
| Angle | What to read | Where we are |
| Length | Single curated list | ~200 slides per year |
| Format | Linkable blog | PDF / Google Slides + survey + compute index + video |
| Bias | Investment-thesis lens (a16z) | Investor + practitioner survey lens (Air Street) |

The two together = the field's "static foundations" + "annual
snapshot." Reading both gives you canon + zeitgeist, which the
homelab's own `llm-end-to-end.md` then layers practitioner-keyed
detail on top of.

## The six recurring dimensions

Every State of AI Report covers the same six categories, which is
what makes the annual archive comparable year-over-year:

| Dimension | What it covers |
|---|---|
| **Research** | Technology breakthroughs, model capabilities, evaluation results, paper highlights |
| **Industry** | Commercial AI deployment, M&A, funding, vendor competitive landscape |
| **Politics** | AI regulation (EU AI Act, US executive orders, China policy), geopolitics of compute |
| **Safety** | Catastrophic-risk identification, red-teaming, alignment progress, incident reports |
| **Survey** | 1,200+ AI-practitioner responses on usage patterns (model preferences, tooling, deployment) |
| **Predictions** | 12-month forecasts, plus prior-year prediction accuracy scoring |

The Predictions section is unusually rigorous for the format — they
score their own previous year's predictions before issuing new ones,
which makes the predictions falsifiable and trackable.

## The archive (2018 → 2025)

All editions live at [stateof.ai](https://www.stateof.ai/) with the
year-specific deck linked from the landing page. Each report is a
self-contained Google Slides deck (~200 slides) plus supporting
materials.

| Year | Notable themes covered (high-level) |
|---|---|
| **2025** | Reasoning models (o-series, R1 family), agent deployment at scale, NVFP4 / Blackwell-era compute, EU AI Act enforcement, increasing weight-export controls, practitioner survey (~1,200 respondents) |
| **2024** | Reasoning paradigm emerging (o1 launch), Llama 3 herd, open-weights closing on closed, multi-agent systems, GenAI compute spending, election-year safety focus |
| **2023** | Generative AI mainstream adoption (post-ChatGPT), foundation-model rush, retrieval as production architecture, regulatory scramble, first wave of AI safety institutes |
| **2022** | Diffusion-models inflection (DALL-E 2, Imagen, Stable Diffusion), Chinchilla scaling law, LLM agents preview, Anthropic & Inflection founded |
| **2021** | Foundation models as terminology (Stanford CRFM paper), AlphaFold 2, large-model concentration in industry vs academia, China–US AI competition foregrounded |
| **2020** | GPT-3 dominates research narrative, "scale is all you need" debate, COVID-era AI applications, MLOps stack consolidation |
| **2019** | Reinforcement learning peak (Dota 2, StarCraft), pre-transformer NLP, autonomous-driving cooldown, hardware-startup landscape |
| **2018** | First edition. Computer vision and supervised learning dominated; transformers barely on the radar. Useful as a "what the field looked like before everything changed" snapshot. |

> **Reading note**: each edition is independently consumable — you
> don't need to read 2018 → 2025 in order. For someone catching up:
> read the most recent year's Predictions + Survey sections first
> (current zeitgeist), then sample older years for "how this prediction
> aged" review.

## What's at stateof.ai beyond the slide deck

The site bundles four supporting artifacts each year:

- **Slide deck** (Google Slides, the canonical artifact)
- **Live survey results** (Typeform dashboard with practitioner responses)
- **Compute Index** (separate resource tracking compute consumption per major lab)
- **25-min video overview** (Nathan Benaich's walkthrough — good
  starting point if you don't want to read the full deck)
- **Blog post** with launch commentary and key takeaways

## A note on what this page is NOT

- **Not a verbatim port** of any single year's report. The reports are
  visual slide decks with charts, embedded media, and live links — they
  don't translate well to markdown reproduction. This page is an
  **index + framing**, not a port.
- **Not a substitute for reading the reports themselves.** The
  reports' value is in the specific data, charts, and predictions per
  year. This page just helps you find them and know what to expect
  before clicking.
